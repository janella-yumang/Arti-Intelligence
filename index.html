AI Basics

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the ability of computers to perform tasks that usually require human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.

How AI Learns

Many AI systems use data to learn patterns. This process is called machine learning. The more quality data and training, the better the model can make predictions.

Why It Matters

AI can improve speed, accuracy, and automation in many fields. At the same time, responsible use is important to reduce bias and protect privacy.

Types of AI

Narrow AI (Weak AI)

Designed for specific tasks like translation, recommendations, image recognition, or voice assistants. Most AI tools used today are narrow AI.

General AI (AGI)

A hypothetical form of AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can do. AGI does not yet exist in practice.

Super AI

A theoretical level where AI surpasses human intelligence in nearly all domains. This remains speculative and is actively debated.

Ethical AI

Ethical AI focuses on fairness, transparency, safety, accountability, and privacy. Responsible design helps ensure AI benefits everyone.
